History of the Buzzer Wire Game

The game was invented in 1953 by Robert Scrimshaw, an English electrician. It started as a garden party attraction and became a popular toy worldwide.

From WWII electronics to carnival games, its evolution is fascinating.

How to Play the Buzzer Wire Game

Guide the metal loop along the wire from start to finish without touching it. If you touch, the buzzer sounds!

Hold the handle steadily and move slowly for best results.

Rules of the Buzzer Wire Game

No touching the wire! Complete the path with minimal buzzes. Variations may include time limits or point deductions.

Standard rule: Zero touches for a perfect score.
